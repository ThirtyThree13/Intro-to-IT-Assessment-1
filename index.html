Interests and Job

Interest in IT

My interest in IT started with my dad. When I was little his job was to program water plant specific parts to create user friendly solutions during the early 2000s and to help modernize the way that water plants operated. From this I grew to enjoy computers and computer games which combined with my interests in cars and lego helped me grow to towards IT as the career that I could build and operate things that interested me. I studied a Diploma of IT at Victoria University prior to starting this course and I chose that course so that I could be flexible to join the Australian Army. I enlisted in 2020 and have since posted to the 1st Combat Signals Regiment in Darwin and I chose to study RMIT because of the recommendations of people at my unit and the flexibility that online study provides.

Ideal Job

My ideal job is my current one. A link to the website is linked below in the references. My current job is as a 661 Information Systems Technician. The role of a 661 can vary depending where in the army you are posted to. The most common posting is to a Combat Signals Regiment which is where you will see the most Sigs as that is a dedicated unit for each of the 3 Army Combat Brigades. This job was appealing to me because of being able to work in the outdoors and IT, having stable employment in different parts of the country so I could explore and see Australia. The army is also very well known for training its personnel and that was what I wanted from an employer early in my career. I have all the required skills, qualifications and experience required for the position which is more based on character than individual skills that one may already have. During the first two years in the army you are taught everything you need to be a Soldier, Sig and SysAdmin on deployed systems. My plan prior to enlisting was to get fit enough to join and study so that I would increase my chance to go into Signals, and my goal of IT work in the army.
